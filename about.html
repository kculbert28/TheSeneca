Who We Are

Founded in 1999 by Harvard undergraduate women, The Seneca, Inc. is a selective women’s community committed to friendship, leadership, and opportunity.

Opportunity, resources, and sustainable networks

The Seneca creates opportunities, resources, and sustainable networks for women in social, educational, and professional environments.

Its work is rooted in the belief that strong communities create lasting impact — both within the organization and across the broader Harvard community.

A selective community with a campus-wide purpose

The Seneca is intentionally small. Each year, only a limited number of new members join, preserving the closeness, trust, and shared responsibility that define the organization.

That selectivity exists alongside a broader mission: many of The Seneca’s programs, partnerships, and initiatives are designed to benefit women across campus, not only its own members.

Diverse, dynamic, and deeply connected

Seneca women bring different backgrounds, perspectives, interests, and ambitions to the organization. That diversity creates a dynamic environment in which members challenge, complement, and strengthen one another.

Within that environment, friendship and collaboration go hand in hand. Members build a strong social community while also working together to expand opportunities and resources for women.
