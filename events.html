Creating Community. Inspiring Change.

The Seneca organizes programs that bring people together, amplify women’s voices, and create opportunities across Harvard’s campus.

Programs that define The Seneca’s public presence

These longstanding events reflect both sides of The Seneca: social tradition, thoughtful programming, and meaningful campus engagement.

Women’s Week

Co-founded in 2007 with the Harvard College Women’s Center, Women’s Week brings together women’s groups across campus for panels, discussions, and events centered on issues affecting women.

Women’s Faculty Reception

Founded in 2010, this reception honors women faculty while creating an elegant setting for students and professors to build stronger relationships.

Women’s Community Fair

Founded in 2006, the fair connects students with organizations, resources, and opportunities focused on women’s involvement and leadership across campus.

The Red Party

Founded in 1999, The Red Party remains one of The Seneca’s signature traditions: a social event created to foster inclusivity, celebration, and connection across the undergraduate community.

Some are public. Some are simply remembered.

Throughout the year, members gather for celebrations, conversations, reunions, and longstanding traditions. Certain traditions are shared publicly. Others remain part of the experience itself.

Shared moments that strengthen bonds

From the Harvard-Yale Tailgate to S-Hop, The Seneca’s traditions bring members, students, and alumnae together in spaces that are celebratory, social, and memorable.

Throughout the year, members also organize internal dinners, discussions, retreats, and gatherings that deepen friendship within the organization itself.
