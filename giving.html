Giving

Investing in friendship, opportunity, and community

The Seneca is sustained by the generosity of its members, alumnae, and supporters. Every contribution helps strengthen both the organization itself and its broader impact on women at Harvard.

What Your Gift Supports

Three core areas of impact

Community & Traditions

Supporting social experiences, gatherings, and longstanding traditions that strengthen friendship and connection within the organization.

Women’s Initiatives & Advocacy

Funding programs and initiatives that raise awareness of women’s issues, expand access to resources, and support women across campus.

Accessibility & Support

Helping ensure membership remains accessible through financial support, community investment, and opportunities that strengthen inclusion within the organization.

Current Philanthropic Focus

Supporting single mothers through professional clothing initiatives

This year, the Via Faciam Foundation — the philanthropic arm of The Seneca — is focusing its efforts on supporting single mothers through clothing drives, fundraising initiatives, and community partnerships centered on professional and everyday workwear.

The organization believes that clothing can play an important role in helping women feel confident, prepared, and empowered as they pursue professional opportunities and provide for their families.

Through donations, fundraising events, and collaborative initiatives, members hope to support women in practical and meaningful ways while strengthening broader conversations around opportunity, dignity, and community support.

The Seneca Capital Campaign

A permanent home near Harvard

The Seneca is working toward the purchase of a property near campus that would serve as a home for its own activities and as a resource for other women’s groups at Harvard.

The vision is to create a permanent space dedicated to connection, collaboration, philanthropy, leadership, and women’s community for generations to come.

Supporters may contribute directly to the campaign or make a pledge to be collected once the total needed to purchase property has been reached.
